01 · The Challenge
01.
A fragmented market and an opaque transaction.

Buyers don't
trust the price
on the page.

Pre-discovery interviews with 18 active homebuyers surfaced the same anxiety in different words: "How do I know this isn't marked up?" Listings showed an asking price, an agent's blurb, and not much else. Days-on-market, prior offers, neighborhood trends — all of it lived in tools the buyer would never see.

Existing real-estate apps optimized for the agent's funnel: contact forms above the fold, scarcity badges, urgency timers. The buyer was an afterthought. Our team's brief was direct: give buyers the same view the agent has.

"It felt like I was negotiating in the dark — every conversation was a guess at whether the number on the page was real." — P07, first-time buyer · Atlanta, GA

02 · The Process
02.
Thinking transparency — every decision shown, not told.

Three rounds
of usability,
nothing precious.

We started with the listing page, the highest-stake screen in the app. I drew it three different ways before writing a single line of copy: a data-led variant (numbers first), a story-led variant (photos first), and a hybrid. We tested all three with five participants per round.

Method
A loop, not a line. Each step fed the next.
01.

Heuristic audit of incumbents

Mapped six competing apps against Nielsen heuristics; identified 14 patterns that prioritized agent intent over buyer intent.

02.

Generative interviews

Eighteen 60-minute sessions with active buyers, ranging from first-timers to third-purchase. Coded for jobs, not features.

03.

Sketch — three directions

Data-led, story-led, hybrid. Drawn fast on paper to defer attachment; each was a deliberate over-correction in one direction.

04.

Mid-fi prototype · Round 1

Five participants. Story-led won on warmth, data-led won on trust; hybrid lost both. We killed the hybrid.

05.

Hi-fi prototype · Round 2

Layered the transparency score and price-history sparkline into the story-led variant. Tested with five more participants.

06.

Pilot release · 3 markets

Shipped a feature-flagged beta to 800 users in Atlanta, Denver, and Raleigh. Iterated against analytics + ten follow-up calls.

03 · The Impact
03.
What changed, what stuck, what we'd do again.

The app made
the search so easy
and fun.

Six months after the pilot, HHI's listing page is the highest-rated screen in the app. The transparency score — initially the riskiest feature on the roadmap — is now cited in 73% of App Store reviews mentioning trust.

Internally, the impact was sharper. The same buyer-research that fed the listing page redirected the entire product roadmap: three planned agent-funnel features were quietly killed, and a buyer-side comparison tool moved up two quarters.

Takeaways

Defer attachment. The hybrid mock was the one I wanted to make work; killing it after Round 1 saved a month.

Show the data, then frame it. The transparency score was inert as a raw number — useful as a letter grade with a single-sentence "why".

Anticipate the second move. The compare flow was added in Round 2 only because three of five buyers tried to compare listings before they could open a second tab.

Next moves

Q3 ships an offer-builder that pulls the same data forward into a negotiation worksheet. Q4 ships transparency scores for the agent side — the same trick, played from the other chair.
